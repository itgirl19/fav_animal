The Gray Wolf

Info

The gray wolf or grey wolf (canis lupus) is also known as the Timber wolf or Western wolf.
The gray wolf is one of the world's best-known and most-researched animals.
The fur comes in alot of different colors that ranges from almost pure white through various shades of blond, cream, and ochre to grays, browns, and blacks.
A group of wolves is called a pack, a pack is a family of 7-8 wolves with a mom, dad, and offspring.

Habitat

The gray wolf is a habitat generalist, and can live in deserts, grasslands, forests and arctic tundras.
Habitat use by gray wolves is strongly correlated with the abundance of prey, snow conditions, absence or low livestock densities, road densities, human presence and topography.
They mostly avoid human areas.

Diet

Although wolves primarily feed on medium to large sized ungulates, they are not fussy eaters.
Smaller sized animals that may supplement the diet of wolves include marmots, hares, badgers,
foxes, weasels, ground squirrels, mice, hamsters, voles and other rodents, as well as insectivores.
They frequently eat waterfowl and their eggs.
